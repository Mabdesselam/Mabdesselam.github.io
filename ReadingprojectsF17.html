Math 7320, Fall 2017, Real Analysis and Linear Spaces II

Proposed reading projects.

Project 1:

There is an analogue of spherical coordinates for integration over matrices. This relates to the vast and active area known as random matrix theory. As an introduction to this subject, I propose for this project to read the article by A. Zvonkin, Matrix integrals and map enumeration: an accessible introduction. Combinatorics and physics (Marseilles, 1995). Math. Comput. Modelling 26 (1997), no. 8-10, 281-304.

Project 2:

Read the article by Cohn and Elkies, New upper bounds on sphere packings. I. Ann. of Math. (2) 157 (2003), no. 2, 689-714. This relates the theory of Fourier transforms of functions in Schwartz space S to optimal sphere packings. We will soon cover Fourier analysis but you can already look up Section 8.3 of Folland to get started.

Project 3:

Read the article by S. Mallat, Multiresolution approximations and wavelet orthonormal bases of L2(R). Trans. Amer. Math. Soc. 315 (1989), no. 1, 69-87. It is about wavelets and Fourier analysis.
Sold! to Mark.

Project 4:

For an introduction to quantum field theory from the operator theory angle read Section IX.8 "The Gårding-Wightman axioms" (with its appendix) and Section X.7 "Free quantum fields", from volume 2 of the book by Reed and Simon "Methods of Modern Mathematical Physics" (1975 edition). This needs some knowledge of spectral theory.
Sold! to Kristin.

Project 5:

About combinatorics and the "game of centipedes", read the first few chapters of the book by Grace and Young "The Algebra of Invariants". I would have to give some one-on-one explanations to relate it to the diagrammatic language. It is very elementary but goes into very deep mathematics.

Project 6:

We will soon see in the present Section 2.2 of the course, the spaces D and s_0 tensor s. An important theorem due to Valdivia and Vogt says that these two topological vector spaces are isomorphic. This project asks to read the nice explicit proof given by Bargetz, Explicit representations of spaces of smooth functions and distributions. J. Math. Anal. Appl. 424 (2015), no. 2, 1491-1505.

Project 7:

A generalization of the method for solving the Poisson Equation is provided by the Malgrange-Ehrenpreis Theorem. This project asks to read the nice article about this result by Ortner and Wagner, A short proof of the Malgrange-Ehrenpreis theorem. Functional analysis (Trier, 1994), 343-352, de Gruyter, Berlin, 1996.
Sold! to Sarasij.

Project 8:

Related to number theory and Tate's Thesis, read Chapter 2 from the Ph.D. thesis of Ajay Chandra. This gives a classification of homogeneous rotation invariant distributions over the reals and the p-adics. Integrating against suitable test functions and taking the product over places, gives L-functions or zeta functions.
Sold! to Stephen.

Project 9:

Regarding Littlewood-Paley theory and Besov spaces, read the article by Gubinelli, Imkeller and Perkowski, Paracontrolled distributions and singular PDEs. Forum Math. Pi 3 (2015), e6, 75 pp. (Read what you can starting from Appendix A)

Project 10:

Related to the previous project, read the article by Hairer, Introduction to regularity structures. Braz. J. Probab. Stat. 29 (2015), no. 2, 175-210. These notes by Joscha Diehl can also help.
Sold! to Liaosha.

Project 11:

Regarding differential operators, read the proof of Peetre's Theorem given by Paul Garrett in the note "Characterization of differential operators".
Sold! to Matt.

Project 12:

Related to number theory and dynamical systems, read the article by Muñoz and Pérez Marco, Unified treatment of explicit and trace formulas via Poisson-Newton formula. Comm. Math. Phys. 336 (2015), no. 3, 1201-1230.

Project 13:

Related to number theory, read the article by Burnol, The Explicit Formula in simple terms.
Sold! to Richard.

Project 14:

Related to number theory, read the article by Connes Trace formula in noncommutative geometry and the zeros of the Riemann zeta function. Selecta Math. (N.S.) 5 (1999), no. 1, 29-106.

Project 15:

Related to Fourier analysis but in a discrete noncommutative setting: read a substantial portion of the book by Persi Diaconis "Group Representations in Probability and Statistics" or the one by Audrey Terras "Fourier Analysis on finite Groups and Applications".
Sold! to Mojdeh (Diaconis book). Someone can still do the Terras book but would have to go quite far since it is a very easy read.

Project 16:

A more ambitious version of Project 3, read the article by Daubechies, Orthonormal bases of compactly supported wavelets. Comm. Pure Appl. Math. 41 (1988), no. 7, 909-996.
Sold! to Vishal.

Project 17:

About the Bernstein polynomial (related to Projects 7 and 8), read the note by Paul Garrett "Bernstein's analytic continuation of complex powers of polynomials".

Project 18:

About Fourier analysis of distributions and the problem of pointwise multiplication, read the article by Brouder, Dang and Hélein, A smooth introduction to the wavefront set. J. Phys. A 47 (2014), no. 44, 443001, 30 pp.

Project 19:

Related to quantum field theory, combinatorics, and the problem of extension of distributions. Read the article by Hairer "An analyst's take on the BPHZ theorem".

Project 20:

Related to Besov spaces and probability, read the article by Furlan and Mourrat, A tightness criterion for random fields, with application to the Ising model. Electron. J. Probab. 22 (2017), paper no. 97, 29 pp. This requires prior exposure to Brownian motion and the Kolmogorov-Chentsov continuity theorem.
